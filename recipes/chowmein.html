Chowmein


A Nepalese–Chinese style stir‑fried noodle dish — quick, savory, and versatile.Chowmein pairs springy noodles with crunchy vegetables and a savoury sauce,
served as street food, home comfort, or a party dish. Can be made vegetarian, with eggs, or with chicken/beef/pork.

Introduction

Tender yet slightly chewy noodles tossed in a glossy, umami‑forward sauce with garlic, ginger, and scallions.
Vegetables are cooked crisp‑tender so the dish has a contrast of textures; flavors balance soy saltiness, a touch of sweetness, mild acidity, and optional heat. Best when cooked on very high heat for a slight smoky wok flavor.

Ingredients(4 serves)

  • 400 grams fresh or dried egg noodles(or thin wheat noodles/spaghetti)
  • 2-3 tablespoon vegetable oil(plus a little for tossing cooked noodles)
  • 2-3 cloves garlic,minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger,minced
  • 1 small onion,thinly sliced
  • 1 cup cabbage shredded
  • 1 medium carrot julienned
  • 1 bell pepper,thinly sliced(optional)
  • 3-4 green onions,sliced(separated white and green parts)
  • 300 gram protein: shredded chicken/thinly sliced beef/tofu/eggs(optional)
  • Sauce:
    • 3 tablespoon light soy sauce
    • 1 tablespoon dark soy sauce(for color, optional)
    • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce(or vegetarian mushroom sauce)
    • 1 tablespoon sugar
    • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar or a splash lemon
    • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
    • 1-2 tablespoon water or stock
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Optional: chili sauce or flakes to taste cornstarch slurry(1 tablespoon cornstarch + 2 tablespoon water) for a slighty saucier finish

Instructions

  1. Prepare noodles
    cook noodles according to package until just al dente.
    Drain, rinse in cold water (for dried noodles) or toss with a little oil to prevent sticking. Set aside.
  2. Mix sauce
    combine soy sauces, oyster sauce, sugar, vinegar, sesame oil, and water/stock. Adjust salt and heat.
  3. Heat wok/skillet
    heat a wok over high heat until very hot. Add oil and swirl.
  4. Cook protein
    add protein and stir‑fry until nearly done. Remove and set aside.
  5. Stir-fry aromatics and veg
    add a touch more oil if needed, sauté garlic, ginger, and white parts of green onion briefly,
    then add onion, carrot, cabbage, and bell pepper. Stir‑fry 2–3 minutes until crisp‑tender.
  6. Combine
    return protein to wok, add noodles and pour sauce over.
    Toss vigorously to coat, using tongs or two spatulas. If using, add cornstarch slurry to thicken slightly.
  7. Combine
    return protein to wok, add noodles and pour sauce over.
    Toss vigorously to coat, using tongs or two spatulas. If using, add cornstarch slurry to thicken slightly.
  8. Finish
    toss in green parts of scallions, adjust seasoning with salt, pepper, or extra soy. Drizzle a little sesame oil,
  9. Serve
    garnish with extra scallions or fried shallots and sever immediately with chilli sauce on the side.
