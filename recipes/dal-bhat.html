Chowmein


Dal Bhat is the staple Nepali meal - a comforting plate of steamed rice(bhat) served with a flavorful lentil soup(dal),seasonal vegetable
curry(tarkari), and achar(pickle). It's a balanced, everyday nutritious,economical and central to Nepali home cooking.

Introduction

The dish centers on soft steamed rice paired with spiced and slightly soupy dal(lentils shimmered with turmeric and finished with a hot tempering/tadka).Accompanying tarkari is a simple sautéed
or curried meat/egg complete the plate.Textures range from silky dal to tender rice and crisp-tender veggies, with layers of warm, aromatic spices.

Ingredients(4 serves)

  • Rice(bhat)
    • 2 cups long-grain rice(washed)
    • 3–3¼ cups water
  • Dal
    • 1 cup split red lentils(masoor) or yellow split pigeon peas(toor/tuvar)
    • ½ teaspoon turmeric
    • 3 cups water(adjust)
    • salt to taste
  • Dal tempering(tadka)
    • 2 tablespoon ghee or vegetable oil
    • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
    • 2–3 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
    • 1 small onion, finely chopped (optional)
    • 1–2 dried red chilies or 1 fresh green chili
    • ½ teaspoon ground coriander (optional)
    • Handful chopped cilantro for garnish
  • Tarkari(simple mixed vegetable curry)
    • 2 tablespoon oil
    • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
    • 1 small onion,sliced
    • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
    • 2 tomatoes, chopped(or 1 tablespoon tomatoes paste)
    • 3-4 cups mixed vegetables(potato,cauliflower,carrot,green beans,spinach)
    • 1 teaspoon turmeric,1 teaspoon cumin,½–1 teaspoon chili powder, 1 teaspoon coriander powder
    • Salt and water as needed
  • Optional
    • achar/pickle
    • fried green chilies
    • papad
    • ghee for serving
    • a small portion of meat or egg for non vegetarian variation

Instructions

  1. Cook rice
    rinse rice until water runs clear. Combine with measured water in a pot (or rice cooker), bring to a boil
    , reduce heat, cover and simmer until water is absorbed and rice is tender (about 12–15 min). Fluff and keep warm.
  2. Cook dal
    rinse lentils. In a pot or pressure cooker, combine lentils, turmeric, and water; simmer until soft and breaking down (20–30 min on stovetop,
    ~1–2 whistles in a pressure cooker). Mash lightly for creamier texture. Season with salt.
  3. Make tadka for dal
    heat ghee/oil in a small pan, add cumin seeds until they sputter, add garlic, dried chili, and onion;
    sauté until golden. Pour the hot tempering over the cooked dal, stir, and garnish with cilantro.
  4. Make tarkari
    heat oil in a pan, add cumin, sauté onions until soft, add ginger‑garlic paste, then tomatoes and spices. Add harder vegetables first (potatoes,
    carrots), add water, cover and simmer until tender. Add quick-cooking greens or peas near the end. Adjust seasoning.
  5. Assemble and serve
    place a scoop of rice on the plate, ladle dal alongside (or over) rice, add a portion of
    tarkari, and serve with achar/pickle and optional papad or ghee. Serve hot.
